Our Methodology

01

Data Collection

Gathering light curve data from NASA's Kepler, K2, and TESS missions, including confirmed exoplanets, candidates, and false positives.

02

Preprocessing

Cleaning and normalizing data, handling missing values, and extracting relevant features from transit light curves.

03

Model Training

Training deep learning neural networks with optimized hyperparameters to detect exoplanet signatures in light curves.

04

Validation

Testing model performance on validation sets and achieving 89.16% accuracy in exoplanet detection.

05

Deployment

Deploying the model for real-time analysis of new astronomical data from ongoing missions.

06

Continuous Improvement

Iteratively improving the model with new data and feedback from the scientific community.

Our Mission & Vision

At ROBO_SPACE, we believe that artificial intelligence holds the key to unlocking countless discoveries in the vast expanse of space. Our mission is to develop state-of-the-art machine learning models that can accurately identify exoplanets from complex astronomical data, accelerating the pace of discovery and contributing to humanity's quest to find potentially habitable worlds beyond our solar system.

We envision a future where AI-powered systems work alongside astronomers to analyze massive datasets in real-time, discovering new exoplanets faster than ever before and potentially identifying the next Earth-like world that could harbor life.
